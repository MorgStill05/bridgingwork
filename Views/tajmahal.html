The Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, Crown of the Palace, is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the southern bank of the river Yamuna in the Indian city of Agra. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his favourite wife, Mumtaz Mahal. It also houses the tomb of Shah Jahan. The tomb is the centrepiece of a 17-hectare complex which includes a mosque and a guest house and is set in formal gardens bounded on three sides by a crenelated wall.
